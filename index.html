I am currently really interested in the intersection of economics and artificial intelligence. I co-developed the Generalized Attribute Based Ratings Information Extraction Library (GABRIEL), a Python library for feature extraction and quantification from text using large language models. I used GABRIEL as part of my senior thesis at Harvard, advised by Prof. Andrei Shleifer, to study a database of over 150,000 full-text sermons given throughout the United States over a twenty year period. The thesis showed how sermons respond to demand from the congregation. While an undergraduate, I worked with Prof. David Yang on Chinese investment in Africa, Prof. Myrto Kalouptsidi on maritime trade disruptions during Covid, and Prof. Michael Szonyi on land acquisition in pre-modern China.

I love learning languages and traveling. In the past, my work has taken me to Morocco - where I used machine learning to understand food security in Africa at the Policy Center for the New South - Argentina - where I presented my work on AI at the South American Business Forum - and Brazil (virtually), where I was a United States Department of State Critical Language Scholar. I also worked with the Macro team at the DE Shaw Group in New York City, focusing on developing uses of large language models for macroeconomic analysis.

While at Harvard, I co-founded the Emerging Technology Group, Harvard's leading student organization focused on the impact of frontier technologies on society. I also served as Co-President of the Charles River Economics Labs (CREL), a student club that has partnered with the State Department, UNDP, EPA, and other organizations to conduct pro-bono economics research. I was also an avid debater at the Harvard College Debating Union. I was an undergraduate prize fellow at the Center for History and Economics and the two-time first place winner of the University of Chicago Econometrics Game.
