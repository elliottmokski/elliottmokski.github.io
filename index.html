Elliott Mokski

Hi there! I am a senior at Harvard College double concentrating in Applied Mathematics in Economics and Computer Science, and East Asian Studies. I grew up in France, the United States, and Germany. I am currently really interested in the intersection of economics and artificial intelligence.

I co-developed the Generalized Attribute Based Ratings Information Extraction Library (GABRIEL), a Python framework for feature extraction and quantification from text using large language models. I am using the framework as part of my senior thesis, advised by Prof. Andrei Shleifer, to understand sermons and religion speech in the United States respond to shocks like the financial crisis and Covid. I have also worked with Prof. David Yang on Chinese investment in Africa and Prof. Myrto Kalouptsidi on the trade disruptions during Covid.

I love learning languages and traveling. In the past, my work has taken me to Morocco - where I worked used machine learning to understand food security in Africa at the Policy Center for the New South - Argentina - where I presented my work on AI at the South American Business Forum - and Brazil (virtually), where I was a United States Department of State Critical Language Scholar.

At Harvard, I co-founded the Emerging Technology Group, Harvard's leading student organizing focused on the impact of frontier technologies on society. I also served as Co-President of the Charles River Economics Labs (CREL), a student organization that has partnered with the State Department, UNDP, EPA, and other organizations to conduct pro-bono economics research. I am also an avid debater at the Harvard College Debating Union. I was elected to Phi Beta Kappa as part of the Senior 48 cohort and am an undergraduate prize fellow at the Center for History and Economics.

I am also an incoming Schwarzman Scholar, studying for a Masters in Global Affairs at Tsinghua University.
